PROFILE

Dr. Evgenia Kapassa is an applied Information Systems scholar and Lecturer focusing on data-driven organizational systems, digital platforms, and emerging AIhuman collaboration in knowledge work and decision-support contexts. She holds a Ph.D. in Business Administration and Digital Innovation (focused on blockchain-enabled information systems) and an M.Sc. in Advanced Information Systems. Her research investigates how analytics, algorithmic assistance, and collaborative platforms are integrated into enterprise information systems to support organizational decision making and innovation. Alongside her teaching activity in Management Information Systems and digital business technologies, she serves as a Senior Researcher and Innovation Engineer on multiple European research projects, contributing to system evaluation, data analytics design, and applied platform research.

RESEARCH AGENDA

My research focuses on data-driven organizational information systems, digital platforms, and emerging forms of AIhuman collaboration in decision-support and knowledge work contexts. I investigate how analytics-enabled systems and algorithmic assistance integrate into human workflows across enterprise environments to support collaboration, managerial decision-making, and innovation processes. Methodologically, my work combines applied quantitative modeling. My ongoing research trajectory seeks to develop a program on co-creation between human users and AI-enabled systems, contributing empirical evidence on collaboration behaviors while informing the design and governance of next-generation organizational information systems.
